# Barthes' Myths: Bouvard and Pécuchet

## What did I read?

Roland Barthes: Mythen des Alltags, 286, Suhrkamp

## Why did I read it?

Thinking about Bouvard and Pécuchet and how they failed miserably once they had the resources to do what they thought they could do.
I feel like it's very similar with AI currently: We can now build everything using AI but we still fail at many levels.
I wanted to know how Bouvard and Pécuchet connects to myths again.

## What did I learn?

Barthes writes that the discourse of Bouvard and Pécuchet is something like a second myth.
They use the discourse of a certain kind of Parisian bourgeoisie, which is itself a myth.
Flaubert then uses this circumstance to form a new myth: The myth of the failures of Bouvard and Pécuchet, which arises from taking the first (bourgeoisie) myth as a reality.

## What do I think about it?

Bouvard and Pécuchet are so removed from reality in their Parisian bubble that they mistake it for the real world.
This reminds me of modern survival YouTubers who often fail in the same comical way as Bouvard and Pécuchet, since their online discourse bears no resemblance to actual reality.

## What does it connect to?

I think AI will similarly be used to create something that appears real within a certain discourse but ultimately proves to be a failure.
We already saw this with [Moltbot/Openclaw](https://securityscorecard.com/blog/beyond-the-hype-moltbots-real-risk-is-exposed-infrastructure-not-ai-superintelligence/), which were hastily developed but contain significant security vulnerabilities.
This reveals that the discourse around AI is often detached from actual capabilities—a modern myth. Future failures like these will likely multiply as the gap between hype and implementation widens.
